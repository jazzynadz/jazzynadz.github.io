BookNote app design


Project duration: May 2021

My role: UX designer designing BookNote app from conception to delivery.

My responsibilities: Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.


Project background

BookNote is a new musician booking app. BookNote connects event organizers, promoters, band leaders, and anyone to musicians (or bands and other talents) near them. BookNote also helps users to book talents, finding gig venues near them, and allows users to manage rehearsals and pre-events.



Users

We conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I’m designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was promoters, event organizers, and band leaders who regularly organize events with music (weddings, corporate gatherings, concerts, etc.).

We conducted user research and received feedback from users that we incorporated into user personas. For example, our user persona, Kirana, is a busy band leader who is regularly hired for gigs and weddings. Kirana needs to find and hire session players to join her regular band based on the format requested by her clients



User journey map

Mapping Kirana’s user journey revealed how helpful it would be for users to easily post a gig vacancy

Research also revealed that this user group needs a tool to secure the deals when they have made their bookings, they need something to make sure that the talents they hire will surely show up on time on rehearsals and on the d-days.



Paper wireframes

I began the visualization process by drawing drafts of each screen of the app, based on the user journey. For the home screen, I prioritized a window where users can check their upcoming event. Users also can browse events nearby and featured artists.



Digital wireframes

As the initial design phase continued, I made sure to base screen designs on feedback and findings from the user research.

Based on the research study, users need to manage their event and rehearsals, or other pre-event meetings.



Low-fidelity prototype

Using the completed set of digital wireframes, I created a low-fidelity prototype. The primary user flow I connected was creating an event and posting a gig vacancy


View Low-fidelity prototype


Usability study

We conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1 findings:

  1. users need simpler forms to fill in, while still providing option for adding more comprehensive details
  2. users need an option to create a routine event

Round 2 finding:

  1. users need an alternative to browse the talents first, then book them for an event (event created later)


Mockup

The event details form on the early design incorporate many fields to enter detailed information, but after the usability studies, I simplified the forms and added buttons for users to add more comprehensive details.

The second usability study revealed that users need another alternative on how to use the app to complete booking a musician. To answer the needs, I added an alternative way for users to browse talents first, then book them for an event.



High-fidelity prototype

The final high-fidelity prototype presented cleaner user flows for creating an event and book talents. It also met user needs for an alternative way to browse talents first then proceed with the booking.


View High-fidelity prototype

Accessibility considerations

  1. Provided access to users who are vision impaired through adding alt text to images for screen readers.
  2. Used icons to help make navigation easier.


Impact:

The app helps users get connected: between promoters or band leaders and talents within their area.

One quote from peer feedback: “I’ve never realized before that so many talents are available near me! I would definitely use this app to get connected to the musicians!”


What I learned:

While designing the BookNote app, I learned that the first ideas for the app are only the beginning of the process. Usability studies and peer feedback influenced each iteration of the app’s designs.
