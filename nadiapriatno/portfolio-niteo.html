Niteo Essentials Responsive Website


Project duration: June 2021

My role: UX designer designing Niteo Essentials responsive website from conception to delivery.

My responsibilities: Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.

Project background

Niteo Essentials is a personal care and beauty brand which focuses on producing and selling custom hair care, body care, and skin care based on customer’s order. Niteo’s website is designed to be a platform where users can take quizes to define their special needs and preferences, than complete the purchase order.



Users

Busy shoppers of all genders and backgrounds who want to have customised personal care products (hair care, body care, and skin care).



Research findings

We conducted user research and received feedback from users that we incorporated into user personas. For example, our user persona, Gayatri, is a 28-year old pregnant mom and full-time architect who wants to have personal care products that are safe for her pregnancy. The research revealed that Gayatri was too busy to do her own research about safe ingredients for pregnant moms. Gayatri would like to receive recommendations of pregnancy-safe natural ingredients within the product’s website, based on her body conditions and preferences.



UI Ideation: wireframes and low-fidelity prototype

Here’s how we put our users’ needs first. The process below begins with our initial ideas wireframes, then moves to mockups, and finally to the high-fidelity prototype.


Wireframes

In this simple version, you can see how the designer approaches solving the user’s needs. Users can enter the ordering flow by viewing featured products first, or they can jump right in to the quizes to build their hair/body profile. There are landmarks listed to show where things such as the shopping cart will be. There is a place for a carousel which will display featured products, ingredients, or other seasonal promotions and informations.


Low fidelity prototype

In this version, the digital wireframes from the previous step were connected together into a working prototype. That meant users were able to interact with each part of the design and test the way it worked. In this example, we demonstrate how the various wireframes that make up the pages of our design fit together when each element becomes interactive.


View Low-fidelity prototype


Insights learned

From user research, we learned that there were a significant pain point for users. After the users complete their hair/body quizes, users receive a recommendation of their customized product, but they cannot check the ingredients before proceeding to check out. This makes users unconfident when they were about to continue to payment. We solved this issue by providing a preview page which also contains recommended product ingredients. If the users agree, they can proceed to check out. If not, they can go back to the previous quizes.



Revised wireframes after low-fidelity prototype usability testing:

In the revised version, users can see their customised product summary and recheck the ingredients, before proceed to check out.



Mockup

Now the design really begins to take shape: actual text is used, colors are applied, and images are added. This mockup shows a visual that gives a better idea of the final design.


Accessibility

The visual design of Niteo Essentials website uses hierarchical headings to order elements on the pages and highlights them by their importance.



High-fidelity prototype

The design is fully developed and gives a complete picture of the completed design. It addresses the user’s needs for a simple design which helps the user’s flow in creating customised products.


View High-fidelity prototype

Conclusion

While designing Niteo Essentials responsive website, we learned that every screen format plays a vital role in user's experience. What we can do best to meet every user's need with their different choice of media is to develop user's interface design with the screen format considerations



Possible next steps

  1. Conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points users experienced have been effectively addressed.
  2. Conduct more user research to determine any new areas of need.
